Solution 1.6.11.1.

There are \(5 \cdot 6^{6}\) functions for which \(f(1) \ne c\) and another \(5 \cdot 6^{6}\) functions for which \(f(2) \ne f\text{.}\) There are \(5^2 \cdot 6^{5}\) functions for which both \(f(1) \ne c\) and \(f(2) \ne f\text{.}\) So the total number of functions for which \(f(1) \ne c\) or \(f(2) \ne f\) or both is
\(\displaystyle{ 5 \cdot 6^{6}+ 5 \cdot 6^{6} - 5^2 \cdot 6^{5} = {272160}. }\)
