Solution 1.5.2.1.

  1. There are \({7 \choose 4}\) numbers. We simply choose 4 of the 7 digits and once chosen put them in increasing order.
  2. This requires stars and bars. Use a star to represent each of the 4 digits in the number, and use their position relative to the bars to say what numeral fills that spot. So we will have 4 stars and 6 bars, giving \({10 \choose 6}\) numbers.
