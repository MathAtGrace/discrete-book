1. \(2^{14} = {16384}\) choices. You have two choices for each tie: wear it or don’t.
  2. You have 511 choices for regular ties (the \(2^{9}\) choices less the “no regular tie” option) and 31 choices for bow ties (\(2^{5}\) total minus the “no bow tie” option). Thus total you have \({511} \cdot {31} = {15841}\) choices.
  3. \({9\choose 3}{5\choose 2} = {840}\) choices.
  4. Select one of the 2 bow ties to go on top. There are then 4 choices for the next tie, 4-1 for the tie after that, and so on. Thus \(2\cdot 4! = {48}\) choices.
