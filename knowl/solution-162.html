Solution 1.7.5.1.

  1. \(8^{9}\) numbers.
  2. \(8^{8} \cdot 4\) numbers (choose any digits for the first 8 digits - then pick either an even or an odd last digit to make the sum even).
  3. We need 5 or more even digits. 5 even digits: \({9 \choose 5}4^5 4^{9-5}\text{.}\) 5+1 even digits: \({9 \choose (5+1)}4^{5+1} *4^{9-5-1}\text{,}\) etc. By adding these together, we get the total number of ways to have 5 or more even digits.
