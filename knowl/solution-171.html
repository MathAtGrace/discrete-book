Solution 1.7.14.1.

There are 9 spots to start the word, and then there are \(8!\) ways to arrange the other letters in the remaining three spots. Thus the number of words avoiding the sub-word “bad” in consecutive letters is \(11! - 9\cdot 8!={3.95539\times 10^{7}}\text{.}\)
If we now need to avoid words that put “b” before “a” before “d”, we must choose which spots those letters go (in that order) and then arrange the remaining 8 letters. Thus, \(11! - {11 \choose 3}8!={3.3264\times 10^{7}}\) words.
