Solution 1.2.9.1.

To get an \(x^{6}\) from the first term, we must pick 6 of the 18 factors to contribute an \(x\text{,}\) leaving the other 12 to contribute a 2. There are \({18 \choose 6}\) ways to select these 6 factors, so the coefficient will be \({18 \choose 6} *2^{12}\text{.}\) Now we must choose 2 of the factors in the second term to contribute an \(x\text{,}\) leaving the other 19 terms to contribute a 3. This gives us \({21 \choose 2}*3^{19}\) for the coefficient resulting from this term. In total, the term containing an \(x^{6}\) will be \({18 \choose 6} *2^{12} + {21 \choose 2}*3^{19}= {2.44151\times 10^{11}}\text{.}\)
