Solution 1.3.9.1.

First, decide where to put the “i”s. There are 12 positions, and we must choose 3 of them to fill with an “i”. This can be done in \(\binom{12}{3}\) ways. The remaining 9 spots all get a different letter, so there are \(9!\) ways to finish off the anagram. This gives a total of \(\binom{12}{3}\cdot 9!\) anagrams. Strangely enough, this is 7.98336E+07, which is also equal to \(P(12,9)\text{.}\) To get the answer that way, start by picking one of the 12 positions to be filled by the first non-”i” letter, one of the remaining 7 positions to be filled by the next, and so on. Then put “i”s in the remaining 3 positions.
