Solution 0.3.6.1.

For \(C \subseteq A\) and \(C \subseteq B\text{,}\) we must have that \(C \subseteq A \cap B\text{.}\) This means that the largest \(C\) could be would be to equal \(\newcommand{\N}{\mathbb N}\newcommand{\st}{:}A \cap B = \{n \in \N \st 22 \le n \le 25\}\text{,}\) which contains 4 elements.
