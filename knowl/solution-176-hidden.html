1. \({12 \choose 7}={792}\) combinations. You need to choose 7 of the 12 cookie types. Order doesn’t matter.
  2. \(P(12, 7) = 12 \cdot (12-1) \cdot (12-2) \cdot \dots \cdot (12-7+1)= {3.99168\times 10^{6}}\) ways. You are choosing and arranging 7 out of 12 cookies. Order matters now.
  3. \({31 \choose 20}={8.46723\times 10^{7}}\) choices. You must switch between cookie type 11 times as you make your 20 cookies. The cookies are the stars, the switches between cookie types are the bars.
  4. \(12^{20}\) choices. You have 12 choices for the “1” cookie, 12 choices for the “2” cookie, and so on.
  5. \(12^{20} - \left[{12 \choose 1}(12-1)^{20} - {12 \choose 2}(12-2)^{20} + \cdots - {12 \choose 12}0^{20} \right]={1.96878\times 10^{20}}\) choices. We must use PIE to remove all the ways in which one or more cookie type is not selected.
