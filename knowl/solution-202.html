Solution 2.2.4.1.

  1. \({n-\left(-1\right)+1}\) terms, since to get -3 using the formula \(3n+0\) we must use \(n=-1\text{.}\)
  2. \(3n - 3\text{,}\) which is 3 less than \(3n+0\) (or plug in \(n-1\) for \(n\)).
  3. \(\frac{(3n - 3)*({n-\left(-1\right)+1})}{2}\text{.}\) Reverse and add. Each sum gives the constant \(3n - 3\) and there are \({n-\left(-1\right)+1}\) terms.
